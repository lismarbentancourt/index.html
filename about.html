☕ Coffee Shop Mini Simulation — by Lismar Bentancourt (2025)

Overview

This mini-simulation helps students practice basic business decisions (pricing, staffing, inventory, marketing) and see how choices affect profit, service time, and satisfaction. It is for educational purposes only.

Features

  • Three difficulty levels (easy / medium / hard) that change demand and costs.
  • Controls for price, staff, inventory, hours, and promo budget.
  • Daily results with revenue, costs, profit, and customer satisfaction.

Tips for Students

  • Start small, track results, and change one variable at a time.
  • Watch wait time and stockouts; they hurt satisfaction and future demand.
  • Balance margin (price) with volume (demand).

Educational Purpose

Applied learning of basic operations/marketing/HR trade-offs; critical thinking under constraints; simple data-driven decisions.

Example Learning Activities & Goals

  • Goal: reach a target profit with ≥80% satisfaction.
  • Activity: compare two pricing strategies for 7 simulated days and explain the trade-offs.

Playing the Simulation

Click Play Simulation at the top to open index.html. Choose a difficulty, set your decisions, run the day, and review the metrics to improve next day.
